? WiFi signal strengthMeasured in dBm (decibel-milliwatts). Range: –30 dBm (excellent, right next to router) to –70 dBm (weak, near edge of range). Anything below –70 dBm is unreliable. The gauge shows the signal quality percentage. On Ethernet, this metric is not applicable and will show –.
Signal Strength
–
? Frequency bandThe radio frequency used by your WiFi connection. 2.4 GHz = longer range, lower speed, more interference. 5 GHz = shorter range, faster speeds. 6 GHz = WiFi 6E, very fast but limited range. On Ethernet, this is not applicable.
Band
–
? Link rateThe negotiated data rate between your device and the access point (e.g. 866 Mbit/s). This is the theoretical maximum for the current WiFi link – actual throughput is always lower due to overhead, interference, and distance. Not applicable on Ethernet.
Link Rate

? Signal levelWiFi signal strength in dBm. Higher (closer to 0) is better: –30 to –50 dBm is excellent, –50 to –60 is good, –60 to –70 is fair, below –70 is weak and may cause dropouts. Not applicable on Ethernet.
