? WiFi signal strengthMeasured in dBm (decibel-milliwatts). Range: –30 dBm (excellent, right next to router) to –70 dBm (weak, near edge of range). Anything below –70 dBm is unreliable. The gauge shows the signal quality percentage. On Ethernet, this metric is not applicable and will show –.

? WiFi network name (SSID)The name broadcast by your WiFi access point or router. Used to identify which network you are connected to. Not applicable on Ethernet.

? WiFi protocol versionThe IEEE 802.11 wireless standard in use (e.g. WiFi 7 = 802.11be, WiFi 6 = 802.11ax). Determines maximum theoretical speed and supported features. Not applicable on Ethernet.
Standard

? Frequency bandThe radio frequency used by your WiFi connection. 2.4 GHz = longer range, more penetration, slower speeds. 5 GHz = shorter range, faster speeds. 6 GHz = WiFi 6E. Not applicable on Ethernet.
Band
–
? Link rateThe negotiated data rate between your device and the access point. This is the theoretical maximum for the current WiFi link – actual throughput is always lower. Not applicable on Ethernet.
